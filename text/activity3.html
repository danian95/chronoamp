The chronoamperometry experiment outlined in the introduction monitored the current following a single potential pulse. In a double-step chronoamperometry experiment we follow the initial pulse and measurement of current with a second pulse—the double in double-step—that returns the potential to its initial value.

Studying Systems of Coupled Reactions

Double-step chronoamperometry is used to study systems of reactions that include a redox reaction and a chemical reaction. Such a system of reactions is called coupled. For example, the reversible one electron reduction of an oxidized species to a reduced species, \(\ce{Ox} + e^- \ce{\rightleftharpoons} \ce{Red}\), followed by the irreversible chemical reaction of the reduced species to form a non-redox active product, \(\ce{Red} \ce{\rightarrow} \ce{Z}\), is an example of coupling a reversible redox reaction to an irreversible chemical reaction.
